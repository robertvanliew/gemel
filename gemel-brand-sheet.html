Brand exploration · naming + identity · June 2026

GEMEL

/ˈdʒɛm·əl/ · noun & adjective · "twin, paired" · from Latin gemellus — the same root as Gemini

A real English word almost nobody remembers, meaning exactly what this tool is: two of a kind, joined. Gemel rings are two interlocking rings worn as one. Bars gemel are twin parallel lines in heraldry — which is what a vertical spread is on a chart. And the Gemini glyph is two pillars under one lintel.

The symbol logic

Gemini ♊ → two pillars → Boaz ("in him is strength") + Jachin ("he will establish") → the short leg + the long leg → every spread is twin pillars under a defined roof

The short leg is the strength — it earns the credit. The long leg establishes the floor — it defines the risk. The pillar that pays and the pillar that protects. The temple porch, the zodiac glyph, and the options math all draw the same picture, so the logo only has to draw it once.

Mark — three directions

1 · The Pillar Glyph

Primary mark. The Gemini glyph rebuilt as Jachin & Boaz: solid green pillar = the short leg (strength, the credit); outlined blue pillar = the long leg (establishment, the floor). Lintel above, base below — risk capped on both ends. The logo is a defined-risk spread.

2 · Candle Pillars

The market-native cousin: the two pillars drawn as candlesticks, wicks meeting the lintel and base. Reads as a chart from across the room and as the temple porch up close. Strongest at large sizes — hero art, splash screens, merch.

3 · Gemel Rings

The literal gemel: two betrothal rings interlocked — one for each of you, one for each leg. Quietest of the three and the most personal. Works as an app icon and as a story you tell once and nobody forgets.

Wordmark

Newsreader caps with the M replaced by the pillar glyph — the twin verticals of the letter M are the one place in the word where the symbol already lives. Use the full serif wordmark in quiet contexts; deploy the pillar-M version where the brand should announce itself.

Voice — taglines

Strength, established. Two pillars. One plan. The pillar that pays. The pillar that protects. The coach, not the calculator.

"Strength, established" is the two pillar names translated — Boaz, Jachin — and also the literal mechanics of a credit spread. Lead with it. "The coach, not the calculator" is the positioning line against OptionStrat and the journals; use it where competitors are in the room.

Scale check

Runner-ups, vetted

Boaz

The richest single-name story — strength, the redeemer of Ruth, the left pillar itself. But the lane is occupied: an active payment-processing company runs boaz.financial, and Boaz-named financial consultancies exist. Usable only with a qualifier (Boaz Markets), which dilutes the Robinhood-style punch.

Teomim / Teom

Hebrew for Gemini itself — Mazal Teomim is your actual birth sign in the tradition. Maximally yours, minimally contested. The friction is spelling and pronunciation for a mass-market US audience; "Teom" (twin, singular) softens it and happens to sound like "team." Keep it in reserve.

On clearance: I ran preliminary collision searches in fintech and app stores — Gemel came back clean where Boaz and Ashlar did not — but a brand decision needs a real USPTO/TESS search and ideally a trademark attorney before you put it on anything public. I'm not a lawyer and this isn't legal clearance. On the symbolism: Jachin and Boaz are drawn from the Temple of Solomon and carried through Masonic tradition — the identity treats them as architecture and meaning, not ritual; nothing sacred is reproduced or claimed.
